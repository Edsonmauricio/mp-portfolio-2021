Photo Evidences

During the class, we could see the benefits of DSLR cameras, so I did some trying on taking the same picture with different resolutions and settings.

In the first picture on the left side, the photo aperture is in wider depth of field when setting it up, the ISO is low, so the picture has low noise, and the shutter speed was around 1/250s and in a frozen movement.

In the second picture on the right side, the aperture was high- F22 or Deep DOF, the ISO was high- 6400/ High Noise and the Shutter speed was high too with 1s and a Blur movement.

The place of it was a park near where I am living that have many trees, flowers, and it is all uncovered so I could see the aspect of nature that I wanted to show it through my photographs, so I took the photos from and different principlesof photography, angles and compositions like close-up, angles extremes and I used the rule of thirds for the photographs.

It was very safe to take photographs with no restriction because it is a public place. The photos were taken on a sunny morning and it was possible to take advantage of the natural lighting during the photo sessions and I used in some of the photographs the back lighting style.

I used my phone because with the new technology it gets to be helpful in a way that when I am taking photographs it could be very similar to the DSLR camera, but the advantages are that I don't need to keep changing the camera resolutions and setting like when I am focusing on something and it would just blur the background with my phone. Then would be easier to carry it out in my pocket as the photographs were taken outside it is much safer than having a DSLR camera.
